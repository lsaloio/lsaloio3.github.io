Accessibility is Key

What is accessibility?

When something is accessible, there is equal access and opportunity to people with diverse abilities.

For example, an accessible building has a ramp right beside a staircase so people with wheelchairs can access the second floor just as easily as everyone else.

Wait, but what about accessibility in the digital world?

How can we make digital content accessible?

Understand your diverse audience.

A staircase is a barrier for a person with a wheelchair just like a website image without alternative text is a barrier to a person with visual impairments.

Research accessible design and implement those features into your digital design goals.

What is Digital Accessibility?

Digital accessibility is a design goal that strives to make electronic content – such as websites, applications, and digital documents – to be accessible and usable by everyone, including and especially people with disabilities. People with disabilities are often at a disadvantage because technology is not designed with their needs in mind, but designing for accessibility can address this issue. Digital accessibility aims to remove barriers that prevent people with disabilities from fully participating in the digital world.

To remove barriers, we must design websites and applications that are compatible with assistive technologies, such as screen readers or voice recognition software, and ensure that the content is perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust (POUR) for all users. Examples of digital accessibility features include alternative text for images, closed captions for videos, keyboard accessibility, color contrast, headings and lists, and simple language for readability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I strive for digital accessibility?

For Individuals and Society: People with disabilities are at a disadvantage when they cannot use technology with the same ease as others, making it important to use digital accessibility guidelines to ensure inclusion in the digital world.

For Businesses and Organizations: Accessible digital content also reaches a more diverse audience, which is especially important for businesses that rely on their digital content.

For Law Followers: Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 requires companies and organizations to make their websites digitally accessible.

What is the impact of making things accessible?

The impact of making things accessible promotes inclusivity, expands potential audiences, and fulfills legal obligations. Digital accessibility in particular not only affects people with disabilities, but it also makes using digital content more convenient for those without disabilities. Making digital content accessible ensures that your content is reaching the widest and most diverse audience possible.

Where do I start?

You're in the right place! This website will show you how to apply three important accessibility features that will help you begin making your digital content accessible. These features include: heading levels and lists, alternative text, and color contrast.
